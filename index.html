ABOUT ME.

Hi, I'm Claire!

I’m an aspiring software engineer studying at Rithm School in San Francisco. I’m passionate about building intuitive products in a thoughtful, equitable, and fun way.

I began my career as a transportation planner at Kittelson & Associates, a transportation consulting company. At Kittelson, I was involved in projects related to systemic safety analyses, database and tool development, traffic operations, and emerging technology preparedness studies. In my spare time, I found myself learning Python to create a web scraper to increase efficiencies for a project and reading articles about the role big data will play in the future of transportation.

Through this work, I discovered a love for diving into the data and creating tools and platforms for our clients and communities to interact with. The more I learned about the ways software engineering could be utilized to build better transportation solutions, the more I wanted to pursue a career in this field. This ultimately led me to Rithm, where I am studying to become a full-stack software engineer.

Prior to Kittelson, I graduated from Rice University (go Owls!) with a degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering. While at Rice, I researched Houston’s energy grid by creating a web scraper in Matab, interned one summer at Tesla, was involved in Engineers Without Borders, and volunteered as an interviewer and tour guide in our admissions office.

In my free time, you can find me taking hour-long breaks to puzzle, hopping on a shared bike to go picnic with friends, or raving about my hometown of Santa Barbara, CA.
